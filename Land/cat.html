Cat (Felis catus)

Siamese Cat

The Siamese cat is one of the first distinctly recognised breeds of Asian cat. It derives from the Wichianmat landrace. The Siamese cat is one of several varieties of cats native to Thailand. The original Siamese became one of the most popular breeds in Europe and North America in the 19th century.

Incredibly intelligent, affectionate and opinionated, they are considered the extroverts of the feline world. Inclined to bond strongly to one person, and often very vocal with a loud, low-pitched voice (known as a 'meezer'), the Siamese is very much a 'Marmite' type of cat, appealing to a very specific sort of person!

Siamese cats live up to 15 years on average, some can reach 20 years old. People love Siamese cats because they are sociable, playful, and entertaining. It is possible to train Siamese cats due to their intelligent nature. They can be trained to walk on a harness and do many tricks.

British Shorthair

The British Shorthair is the pedigree version of the traditional British domestic cat, with a distinctively stocky body, thick coat, and broad face. The most familiar colour variant is the "British Blue", with a solid grey-blue coat, pineapple eyes, and a medium-sized tail.

The British is a fiercely loyal, loving cat and will attach herself to every one of her family members. While the British loves to play, she doesn't need hourly attention. If she is in the mood to play, she will find someone and bring a toy to that person.

The British shorthair lifespan is 12 to 20 years. Personality: British shorthairs are friendly, if not overly affectionate. They aren't inclined to cuddle because their thick fur makes close contact feel too warm. They do like to stay nearby when their people are home, though, even if they don't sit on your lap.

Maine Coon

The Maine Coon is a large domesticated cat breed. One of the oldest natural breeds in North America, the breed originated in the U.S. state of Maine, where it is the official state cat. The Maine Coon is a large and social cat, commonly referred to as "the gentle giant.

Maine Coon cats have an affectionate nature and are very playful and friendly. They enjoy human company and this makes them excellent companions for someone who enjoys sharing their home with such an enormous cat. Maine Coon cats are noted for the delightful quiet chirping sound they make.

Maine Coon cats usually live to around 13 or 14 years, but some have been known to live much longer. They are affectionate, intelligent, and sociable, enjoying the company of people without being overly clingy. Their playful and curious nature lasts well into adulthood, and they typically get along well with other pets.

Persian Cat

The Persian cat, also known as the Persian Longhair, is a long-haired breed of cat characterised by a round face and short muzzle. The first documented ancestors of Persian cats might have been imported into Italy from Khorasan as early as around 1620, but this has not been proven.

Persian cat personalities are sweet, gentle, and patient. They're friendly cats that prefer calm environments but can easily adapt to more active households. Persian cats love to relax and lounge around their environment. They have a sweet nature and will sit on your lap or cuddle with you when the mood strikes them.

The Persian cat lifespan is 12 to 18 years. Personality: Persian cats are affectionate and gentle, enjoying a calm and relaxed environment. They prefer lounging over climbing and are content with quiet companionship. While they love being pampered, they are not overly demanding and appreciate a peaceful home.

Ragdoll

The Ragdoll is a breed of cat with a distinct colorpoint coat and blue eyes. Its morphology is large and weighty, and it has a semi-long and silky soft coat. American breeder Ann Baker developed Ragdolls in the 1960s. They are best known for their docile, placid temperament and affectionate nature.

Common Ragdoll cat characteristics include their affectionate demeanor, patience with children, and willingness to play gently — often without ever using their claws. One charming aspect of the Ragdoll cat personality is their tendency to go limp when carried.

The average Ragdoll cat will live between 12 to 15 years. They are gentle, affectionate, and known for their relaxed nature. Ragdolls enjoy human companionship and often follow their owners around the house. Regular check-ups with a vet are essential for maintaining their health and longevity.

Sphynx Cat

The Sphynx cat is a breed known for its lack of fur, giving it a unique and striking appearance. It originated in the 1960s and was selectively bred for its hairless trait. Despite their bald look, Sphynx cats are warm to the touch and require regular skin care.

Sphynx cats are highly affectionate, social, and playful. They enjoy being the center of attention and form strong bonds with their owners. Due to their lack of fur, they seek warmth and love snuggling into blankets or sitting in sunny spots.

The lifespan of a Sphynx cat is typically between 12 to 15 years. Proper care, including regular bathing to remove skin oils and protection from extreme temperatures, helps ensure a healthy and happy life.

Abyssinian Cat

The Abyssinian cat is one of the oldest known breeds, believed to have originated from ancient Egypt. Known for its distinctive ticked coat, this breed is active, playful, and intelligent. Abyssinians are often curious and enjoy exploring their surroundings.

Abyssinians are slender, graceful, and athletic cats. Their coat is soft and dense, with a unique ticking pattern. They are highly energetic and thrive in an environment where they can climb, run, and play. Abyssinians are known for being social, affectionate, and loyal companions.

Abyssinian cats have a lifespan of 12 to 16 years. They tend to be healthy, but they may be prone to genetic conditions such as kidney disease or dental problems. Regular vet visits and a balanced diet can help maintain their health.

American Shorthairs are medium to large-sized cats with a round face and a broad chest. Their short coat comes in a variety of colors and patterns. Known for their easygoing personality, they enjoy being around their families and are not overly demanding of attention.

Burmese Cat

The Burmese cat is a breed of domestic cat, originating in Burma, believed to have its roots near the Thai-Burma border and developed in the United States and Britain.

Curious, clever, and friendly, the Burmese cat is described as having a sweet expression in the breed standard, and has a nature to match if raised and socialised well. Very playful and engaging, the Burmese will learn to retrieve toys, and will delight in playing with their family.

On average, Burmese cats have a lifespan of 10-16 years. However, it's not uncommon for a healthy Burmese cat to live beyond 16 years. According to the Westfield Animal Hospital, a Burmese cat once featured in the Guinness World Records for reaching 35 years of age.

Birman

The Birman, also called the "Sacred Cat of Burma", is a domestic cat breed. The Birman is a long-haired, colour-pointed cat distinguished by a silky coat, deep blue eyes, and contrasting white "gloves" on each paw. The breed name is derived from Birmanie, the French form of Burma.

The Birman cat personality is incredibly charming. They're gentle, loyal pets. These animals do have the curious, outgoing traits that are common to most cats, but they're also very affectionate. They're sometimes called “velcro” cats because they tend to follow their owners around throughout the day.

The Birman cat's lifespan is typical for a feline. They live an average of 9 to 15 years. This means that you should plan on spending a decade with this pet before choosing to adopt one, especially if you get one as a kitten.

Scottish Fold

The Scottish Fold is a distinctive breed of domestic cat characterised by a natural dominant gene mutation associated with osteochondrodysplasia. This genetic anomaly affects cartilage throughout the body, causing the ears to "fold", bending forward and down towards the front of the head.

The Scottish Fold is seemingly made entirely of curves! This rounded, medium sized cat is compact and solid, with a round head, large round eyes and most notably their ears are small and neatly folded forward covering the ear opening. The Scottish Fold has a sweet expression and a luxurious short, dense coat.

The average Scottish Fold lifespan is 14 to 16 years. All Scottish Fold Cats with folded ears are affected by osteochondrodysplasia, also known as Scottish Fold disease. Scottish Fold disease affects cartilage throughout the cat's body and can cause serious issues such as: Arthritis.

Bombay Cat

The Bombay cat is a short-haired breed of domestic cat. Bombays are glossy solid black cats with a muscular build, and have characteristic large bright copper-golden eyes. The breed is named after the Indian city of Bombay, referring to the habitat of the Indian black leopard.

The Bombay is a very solid cat both in looks and in feel, and has great strength. She is a good climber and jumper and should have cat trees and perches. The Bombay is a sturdy, stocky cat and you might have to watch her nutrition carefully to prevent obesity, particularly if she does not get enough exercise.

Bombay cats live between 12 and 16 years, with some living longer with proper care. They are affectionate and enjoy human companionship, but they’re more playful and energetic than Ragdolls. They love interactive play and often follow their owners around the house.

Siberian Cat

The Siberian is a centuries-old landrace of domestic cat in Russia, and recently developed as a formal breed with standards promulgated the world over since the late-1980s. Since 2006, the breed is recognised for registry and championship status with all major cat registries.

Social and intelligent, but independent where necessary, the Siberian Forest cat is a curious and clever problem solver. Despite their huge size, they are agile and athletic, able to make great leaps to reach what they want, whether a hidden toy, a bowl of food left on a worktop or just a desirable vantage point!

Siberian cats weigh between 6.8-9.1 kg for males and 4.5-6.8 kg for females. Known for their sturdy build and thick, water-resistant coat, they are friendly, playful, and affectionate. The average lifespan of a Siberian cat is 10 to 18 years, with proper care helping them live longer.

Russian Blue

The Russian Blue cat, commonly referred to as just Russian Blue, is a cat breed with colors that vary from a light shimmering silver to a darker, slate grey. The short, dense coat, which stands out from the body, has been the breed's hallmark for more than a century.

Russian Blue cats are known for their striking blue-gray coat and bright green eyes. They have a calm, gentle, and affectionate nature but tend to be reserved with strangers. Norwegian Forest Cat

The Norwegian Forest Cat, less commonly referred to simply as the Norwegian Forest, is a breed of domestic cat originating in Northern Europe. This landrace breed is adapted to a very cold climate, with a top coat of long, glossy hair and a woolly undercoat for insulation.

A feline comedian that loves to charm and amuse. Extremely friendly, affectionate but not overly demonstrative. Smart and excellent hunters. Welcomes the company of children, other cats and dogs.

The lifespan of a Norwegian Forest Cat is typically 14-16 years. These cats are known for their robust health, thanks to their natural background. They are friendly, independent, and enjoy outdoor activities, making them great companions for active households.

Devon Rex

The Devon Rex is a tall-eared, short-haired breed of cat that emerged in England during the late 1950s. The breed is known for its atypical appearance, with an oddly shaped head, large eyes, and the short and wavy coat.

The Devon rex is known for their alien-like appearance, marked by large, low-set ears, big eyes, and a triangular face. Aside from striking facial features, they have a thin, wavy coat that comes in a wide range of colors and patterns. The Devon rex is exceptionally active, sociable, and friendly.

Devon rex cats have a lifespan of 9–15 years and are typically healthy pets. The breed's greatest health risks include hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM), hip dysplasia, luxating patella, congenital myasthenic syndrome (a muscle condition), and hereditary baldness.

Munchkin Cat

The Munchkin is a breed of cat characterized by its very short legs, which are caused by a genetic mutation. Compared to many other cat breeds, it is a relatively new breed, documented since 1940s and officially recognized in 1991. The Munchkin is considered to be the original breed of dwarf cat.

They are playful, outgoing and easy to train. They are very sociable and loving cats who make fantastic family pets as they get along well with children and other pets. Munchkins are known to be generally healthy, with an expected lifespan of 12–15 years. Their playful, curious nature makes them fun companions, and despite their short legs, they are quite active and enjoy interacting with their owners. Regular care can help them live a long, happy life.

Manx Cat

The Lykoi is a breed of cat derived from a natural mutation causing a form of hypotrichia found in domestic short-haired cats. The mutation has occurred in domestic cats over the last 20 years. DNA testing has been done by UC Davis to confirm that the cats do not carry the Sphynx/Devon Rex gene.

Inquisitive and intelligent by nature they are keen hunters and even known to play fetch. They are found of company and bond well with their owns, although they may be wary of new people and pets until they get to know them. These affectionate kitties make for loving companions.

The Lykoi cat lifespan is similar to other cat breeds at 10 to 15 years. The Lykoi cat personality is friendly and outgoing, though reserved with strangers at first. Although they are loyal and affectionate, they are more known for playing and exploring than cuddling in laps.

SNOWSHOE CAT

The Snowshoe is a rare breed of domestic cat originating in the United States of America in the 1960s. The Snowshoe is a short-haired bicolour colourpoint breed. Snowshoes were first produced when a Siamese breeder's cat gave birth to three kittens with white feet.

Snowshoe cats are known to get along well with children and other pets. The breed is usually mellow and sweet natured with a sociable personality. These cats thrive under the care of their owners, so they don't fare well if left alone for long durations.

The Snowshoe cat's lifespan is longer than that of the average cat on average, at 14 to 20 years. In terms of the Snowshoe cat personality, the Snowshoe once again exhibits traits from both of its ancestors.

TOYGER

The toyger is a breed of domestic cat, the result of breeding domestic shorthaired tabbies to make them resemble a "toy tiger", as its striped coat is reminiscent of the tiger's.

Toyger personality: Toygers were bred to be companion pets, so they have an easygoing nature. They are usually characterized by positive personality traits such as a pleasant temperament, relaxed personality, intelligence, trainability, and ease of handling. They are content to live among humans, including children.

Toyger cats have a life expectancy of 10 to 15 years. The exact life span of every Toyger will depend on their overall health and whether they are an indoor or outdoor cat.

COLORPOINT SHORTHAIR

The Colorpoint Shorthair is a variety of Siamese cat. The only major registries to recognise them are the Cat Fanciers' Association and the World Cat Federation. This breed was established from breeding American Shorthairs with the Siamese to produce different point colors, beyond the four standard Siamese colors.

The colorpoint shorthair is a human-made breed. They are well-known for their distinct coat patterns, which come in cream, red, lynx, and tortoiseshell points. These cats are loud, loving, and full of personality. They make ideal family companions because of their friendly and outgoing nature.

The average Colorpoint life expectancy is 8-12 years, although some have lived as long as 17 years. Proper care, including a balanced diet, regular veterinary check-ups, and a healthy lifestyle, can help extend their lifespan.

JAVANESE CAT

The Javanese, also known as the Colorpoint Longhair in some registries, is a variety of purebred domestic cat. In the Cat Fanciers' Association, it is an offshoot of the Balinese breed, out-crossed to Siamese, Colorpoint Shorthair, and Balinese cats.

Javanese cats are intelligent, spunky, and playful felines that often seek out your attention. After meeting a Javanese, chances are you can't help but notice their uncanny similarities to the Siamese cat breed. Read on to learn more about this breed's history, personality, and appearance.

Like their other cat relatives, the Javanese breed has point coloration, but their coat can appear in colors and patterns the Siamese and Balinese cannot. The expected lifespan for these felines is 10-15 years.

SOKOKE

The Sokoke is natural breed of domestic cat, developed and standardised, beginning in the late 1970s, from the feral khadzonzo landrace of eastern, coastal Kenya. The Sokoke is recognized by four major cat pedigree registry organizations as a standardised cat breed.

Sokoke cats are very active and enjoy climbing. They tend to be vocal toward human keepers and other cats with whom they live. They bond deeply to each other, as well as their owners. This trait makes re-homing harder for them, with a longer adjustment period expected in adult cats and older, already-bonded kittens.

Typically, a healthy adult Sokoke will weigh between 5-10 pounds, with an approximate life expectancy of 12-15 years. With proper care, including a nutritious diet, regular vet check-ups, and an active lifestyle, they can live a long and healthy life.

AUSTRALIAN MIST

The Australian Mist is a breed of cat developed in Australia. It is a cross between the Abyssinian cat, the Burmese cat, and the Australian Tabby cat. The Australian Mist has a distinct and unique coat pattern hence the name.

The Australian Mist is popular with first-time cat buyers because of its moderate appearance with no extremes. Its eyes are expressive, its face is broad and its cheeks are plump. The cat's thick coat is short and hardy, its tail is plumply furred and the cat is of medium size. The colour scheme is delicate.

This breed of cat is quite docile and friendly and makes a great indoor cat. It is also a safe playmate as a family cat or for elderly companionship. The Australian Mist typically weighs between 4 and 7 kg. The lifespan of the Australian Mist is usually 12 to 15 years.

KHAO MANEE

The Khao Manee cat, or Khao Plort, also known as the Diamond Eye cat, is a rare, natural breed of domestic cat originating in Thailand, which has an ancient ancestry tracing back hundreds of years. They are mentioned in the Tamra Maew. Khao Manee cats are short-haired solid-white cats.

The Khao Manee has a wedge-shaped head, moderately large eyes, and relatively large ears with oval tips. These cats are bred to do well in hot tropical weather and have lithe, muscular bodies with a short coat of hair. The Khao Manee has a striking all-white coat and jewel-toned eyes.

The average Khao Manee lifespan is 10 to 12 years. The Khao Manee is a generally healthy breed. However, purebred white cats like the Khao Manee can be prone to congenital deafness in one or both ears.
